Why was Horus created?

Click-driven monetization, along with other factors, seems to be creating economic incentives for media companies to consider honesty and unbiased accuracy irrelevant. This makes misinformation viral, putting people under sensationalistic enslavement. Retweet

Horus's goal is to help people filter the truth out of uncertaincy.

Why use Horus?

Open Source

Anyone can contribute to Horus. Its source code is publicaly avaliable. Where?

Decentralized

Horus is based on the Interplanetary File System (IPFS) and the Ethereum Blockchain. This makes it more free and censorship resistant. Why?

Truth-centered

Horus's mechanics promote unbiased and evidence-based judgment and make malicious bot frauds useless. How?

Limited and untransferable reputation

Horus is based in a limited-supply, untransferable reputation system. New users get reputation by borrowing it from another user to later return it with interest.

Spam-free post mechanics

Users can posts claims and invest reputation to vote whether a given post is spam and whether it is true. Every post can point to another post to which it claims to be evidence for (in favor or against).

Evidence-based legitimacy propagation

The score of a post is the total score of all the non-spam posts in favor minus the sum of the posts against. Legitimacy is thus propagated from increasingly fundamental facts to complex issues.

Daily reputation profits

Every day a small fraction of the total invested reputation in each post is proportionately distributed to the users who support it if its score is positive or the those against it if its negative.
